Mental Health

What Is Anxiety?

Anxiety is a natural feeling that everyone experiences during time to time. It's a reaction that prepares you to face challenges but sometime the alarm could go off when there's no real problem. It can be overwhelming when it starts to interfere with dailty activities, school, sleep and relationships.

What Can Anxiety Feel Like?

Anxiety can show up in many different ways, it affects your body, behavior and can take over your thoughts. It isn't just a feeling of worry it can physically affect people and cause overthinking. When anxiety starts to get overwhelming, you may find yourself wanting to avoid places such as school, avoid friends and family, or put a lot of pressure on yourself.

Common Signs of Anxiety

  • Feeling constantly on edge
  • Physical aches
  • Lack of focus
  • Sleep disrutions

What Can Trigger Anxiety?

Anxiety is like your body built in alarm system, An upcoming event or stressful life situations may trigger anxiety. Common triggers include public speaking, academic pressure, social media comparasion, relationships could cause anxiety. Everyone triggers are different and one event that feels stressful to one person ay not feel stressful to another.

Ways to Cope

  • Talk to a trusted friend, family member, or professional
  • Breathing excerises to calm your body
  • Create healthy sleeping habits
  • Stay active by exercising and moving more
  • Write your thoughts down in a journal

You're Not Alone

Nearly one in three teens have experienced anxiety, and you're not the only one going through it. Talking to a person you trust may help.

When Should You Reach Out For Help?

Feeling nervous sometimes is normal, you should begin reaching out for support when your anxiety is becoming excessive and begins interfering with your daily activities. When anxiety gets in the way of your academics, relationships/activities or more you can reach out to someone you trust
